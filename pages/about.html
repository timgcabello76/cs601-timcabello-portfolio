About Me

// background, where I've been, and what I'm doing now

Background

I've been working in IT since the early 2000s. Long enough to remember building pages with nested tables, fighting IE6 layout bugs, and treating JavaScript as something you added to a page rather than built with. I started picking up HTML and basic JS on the side while doing infrastructure and systems support work, more out of curiosity than any real plan.

Most of my career has been on the business and systems side rather than in a developer role. Application analysis, IT consulting, decision support work. Over time I got comfortable sitting between technical teams and business stakeholders, understanding both sides well enough to be useful to each. Coding was always in the background, something I could do but never the main focus.

A few years back I decided to go back to school and do this properly. I'm finishing a graduate degree in Computer Information Systems at Boston University Metropolitan College and a Master's in Business and Technology at Purdue at the same time. CS601 this summer is the first time I've gone through modern front-end development in order, from start to finish, with actual structure behind it.

Web development, specifically

I always knew enough HTML and CSS to get something working. Understood the DOM, had opinions about layout, could put a page together when I needed to. The issue is I learned most of it the same way a lot of people do when they're busy and self-taught. Whatever got the job done.

This course has been useful in filling that in. Some of it is new to me, TypeScript, React's component model, Vite, the whole modern tooling ecosystem. Some of it is re-learning things I knew already but with better approaches behind them. Either way it's been worth going through with structure rather than just picking things up as projects required them.

This site is the CS601 term project. HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, TypeScript in places, and React for the projects section. It's a working portfolio and not trying to be anything more than that.

Outside of work and school

I play guitar, have for years. I follow technology out of actual interest rather than just keeping up for professional reasons. And I've been slowly getting through a backlog of TV shows and movies from the 80s, 90s, and early 2000s that I never had time for when they were current. A lot of good stuff from that era that I mostly missed.

Based in Pueblo, Colorado.
