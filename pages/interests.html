Interests

// outside of work and school

♫

Guitar

Been playing for a long time now. Long enough that you'd think I'd be better at it, but that's how it goes. There's something useful about sitting down with an instrument that has nothing to do with a screen. Even on days where it's not going well, it's a different kind of mental space.

I tend to play older rock and blues. The kind of stuff that actually rewards learning the instrument rather than just relying on effects. Still working through songs that felt impossible when I first picked up a guitar. The list doesn't get shorter.

💻

Keeping up with tech

This one overlaps with work, which is fine. I've been following technology closely since the late 90s, not just as something to keep up with professionally but because I find it genuinely interesting to understand how things work and where things are heading.

AI is hard to avoid right now and I don't try to avoid it. I'm specifically interested in the governance and strategy side of it, how organizations actually make decisions around adoption and risk rather than just the technical side. That's partly why the Purdue program made sense to pursue alongside the CS work at BU.

🎞

Classic TV and film

For a long stretch I was too busy to watch much. So there's a pretty significant backlog of shows and movies from the 80s, 90s, and early 2000s that I'm slowly working through. Stuff that everyone else seems to have already seen years ago.

A lot of good material from that era that doesn't come up much anymore because it's not tied to anything currently trending. I find it more interesting than most of what's being produced right now, honestly. Good way to decompress at the end of a long school and work day.

📚

Going back to school

Doing two graduate programs at the same time while running a consulting practice is not something I would describe as easy. But it's been worth it. There's a real difference between knowing how to do something and actually understanding it, and school has a way of forcing the second one.

CS601 is a good example. I've been putting web pages together since before CSS layouts were standard. But going through it properly, with modern tooling and actual feedback, has made me fix some habits I'd had for twenty years. Some of those habits were fine. Some of them were not.
